# Une Faible Impression — Acting Reference

*A guide for how we see the performance. These are not meant to kill your creativity, rather, they are a direction to lean toward.*

---

## The one thing to remember

Don't show us the feeling. Let us catch it.

The camera is going to be patient. It'll wait. You don't have to fill the silence or prove the emotion. Trust that we are watching closely. Imagine you are a real person, alone with a thought. You wouldn't perform the thought.

---

## Face

Always less than you think.

Let the feelings live behind the eyes, not on the mouth. When something moves you, the first instinct is to let the face react. Resist it. Let the moment pass through you and out the eyes. We will see it.

Look at things the way you look at something you are trying to remember. Not searching hard. Letting it come.

## Walking

Walk like the body is somewhere the mind has not caught up to yet. Walk like there are weights tied to you.

No purpose in the steps. Remember, you are not going toward anything. The walking is thinking. Slow, even, a little aimless. The ground matters as much as the destination.

## Voice

Speak as if the other person is already half-listening.

No projection. No selling the line. These two are not really having a debate. They are thinking out loud near each other. The words are heavy but the delivery is light, almost careless.

Let pauses sit. A silence is not dead air. It is the line still working.

The letter (voiceover)

He is writing to his mother. He is alone, and he thinks no one will ever hear this.

Slow, but not solemn. Don't perform grief. The pain is small and it lives underneath the words, not on top of them. Think of someone reading their own handwriting back, quietly, testing whether it's true. The voice catches a little when the feeling gets close, then carries on. He scratches the words out and starts again because the words keep failing him, not because he is overwhelmed.

Keep it private. We are overhearing... The moment he forgets the French word and reaches for it, that reaching is the whole performance in miniature. Let us hear him not quite get there.

## Hands and stillness

The body should mostly be still. When it moves, it should mean something without trying to.

No nervous gestures. No filling space. A cigarette, a book, a map are things to hold, not things to act with. I imagine them being held the way you hold something you have held a hundred times.

Keep it small. Almost nothing.

## The eyes (the closing)

The old woman at the end is not sad. She knows something.

Look into the distance like you are looking at a place you have already been. Not longing for it. Recognizing it.

## The boy

He is listening to something we cannot hear.

Not scared, not happy. Pulled. Curious in the way a child is curious before they learn to be careful. Everything he does is real and nothing he does is planned.
